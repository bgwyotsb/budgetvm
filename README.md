## What BudgetVM Actually Offers

The product range is wider than you might expect from a budget-focused brand. There are four main categories:

**Dedicated Servers** — Physical bare-metal machines, fully customized to your specs. No neighbors hogging your RAM at 3am. Everything from a compact Intel E-2436 entry unit all the way up to dual-socket Xeon Gold configs with 128GB of memory and 10Gbps ports.

**Cloud / VDS Servers** — Virtualized slices of their Intel Gold 6230 infrastructure. Think of these as the sweet spot between a VPS and a full dedicated machine. You get dedicated resources (not shared), NVMe storage, and the same DDoS protection as the bare-metal lineup, but at a lower entry price.

**GPU Servers** — This is where it gets interesting. They stock GTX 1650, RTX 3050, RTX 4090, L40S, single H100 NVL, and dual H100 NVL configurations. For AI workloads, rendering, or blockchain validation, this is a meaningful differentiator over a typical cheap host.

**Storage & Blockchain Servers** — Purpose-built machines for high-density storage and specific chain validators (Solana, Ethereum, Polygon, Avalanche, Cardano, and more).

Everything ships with 1Gbps or 10Gbps bandwidth, 1,800+ Gbps DDoS mitigation, and a 30-day money-back guarantee. The SSAE16-compliant data centers have biometric access, redundant UPS, and generator backup. Basically, the physical stuff you'd hope for, all owned and operated by their parent company — no middlemen.
